Comparative Analysis

Comparative analysis is useful for me to collect and compare data about different products that exist in the marketplace. I can gather useful information on the key user demographic, list of the features and conduct an evaluation of the visual design language. Given there is a wide range of products similar to the one I have created, I will look for comparatives based on creator platforms.

USER PERSONAS

Based on the comparative analysis, I have created four user persona’s that represent the demographics. This outlines the behaviours, needs, goals and I also want to capture the reach the user personas have on social media. I believe this element is important to our users, and should remain central consideration when working through the ideation stage.

BRAINSTORMING

Normally, when I have brainstormed it’s involved thinking in a pro-solution mindset, in other words, what potential alternative solutions can be created that works. However, this time round I want to work with creating a number of alternatives that don’t work, though this process I want to understand why it wouldn’t work, and so gathered information that will allow me to empathise with the user. The aim of this process is to highlight what the actual issues are, before drawing out possible solution during wireframing. I am already challenging my own thinking to prevent bias.

Card Sorting

Reflecting on the previous methods I used for social media research – the thematic process allowed me to find, group and identify patterns. In the same way, I wanted to find a pattern in what user found important in relation to the structure of the information architecture. The reason why I think using card sorting will help because, to me it will show how users will group but also what is prioritised. Card sorting is particularly relevant here, because the solution I am trying to solve involves organise the structure of the end-end timescale visible to the users.

USER FLOW

Between the brainstorming session and card sorting, this has identified the use case for the screens, it shows the menu and options for the user actions and showing the impact of the choices they make. It keeps the focus, and define the scope of what we are working towards and gives me also the chance to visual the design and iterate where necessary before wireframing.

I want to show the iteration process on the Low fidelity wireframing, to see how I have responded to the feedback and changes, and explain my process in detail as it been something I haven’t done in real dept, and something I would like to develop further on.

REFLECTION

I have loved being able to explore and change things as I work through the process. It’s not looking for the ideal situation, it’s saying what have we got and what can we do with it. I have found myself fully embracing this as I have worked on this project, particularly looking at the priorities of the sorting cards. In my own bias, I would have organised based on what I would have thought was logical, and evidently see this was different mine after the evaluation of the task. This allowed me to work with what I was given to determine a different workable solution, which I found the mindset to adopt to be super useful. In alternative approach I decide to look at the creating bad solution for the problem, to understand what would lead to the effective solution. By turning this on its head, got me thinking with two different hats on which I could then go back and forth in the brainstorming session to come up a solution that holds up – this really helped me empathise with the users which I found constructive.
