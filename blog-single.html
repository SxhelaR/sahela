So, are we there yet?

  • by:Sahela Rani
  • date:January 07, 2021

Often the process of creating starts with an idea for a design. Then we made some changes, and more than often it becomes more complex. Ok, once we have done that a few times over, we decide it time to show our work to someone else. They will give their insights and mostly likely we’ll mull over the feedback. Then maybe ask another person if we are not confident with the first piece of feedback. We may decide to share the original idea so they can compare. They come back, and say they really like the latest design. Hoorah! However, there is a niggling feeling that we’re missing something, and we can’t put our finger on it. Curiosity strikes again.

With this in mind, we go back to the first piece of feedback. Makes some changes to see how we feel about it. It seems to be going the right way, something is clearly working. Hmmmm. Now, we need to check this will more than one person. Contact a few friends to see whether they could look at it. Emails sent. Before they even respond back – we are attempting to work out what they might say. Opens the internet browser, we are looking for some further inspiration.

No such thing as an end design, just one that allows for more changes

About 8pm, they get back to us. The feedback was on average good with additional comments about colour, arrangement, and text. Mostly predicted but there is a one comment we did not consider. Great! How do we fit this in? We continue to google and play about until we found a way that works. Feeling quite satisfied, we continue to build out the design – we are in a flow. The colour and theme are starting to form now.

We move on to functionality – we want people to use this. We think to ourselves, what is the most logical pattern in human behaviour that works with this design. We get a piece of paper, and jot down various forms of logic on user flow. A few attempts later, a couple of scribbles here and there – found that one most logical to us. Now we have a functional idea with a design. Now we need to test this with a far bigger audience. How? We deploy the idea online, allow people to test it. Collect the feedback once they’ve use it. Now, this time, we are not completely sure what we might expect. We take some time off until everything is collected and reviewed….

Ok, so nearly there. Read through the comments, some rather interesting points. To know which one works best, we decided to test the logic. Noting down at the same time on how easy it is to more through the design. Ranking each one, including both positive/ negative comments. It’s seems we have our top three logical pathway we can test to our users. Test, test, test again! Interestingly, there clearly seems to be a frontrunner here.

Ah, but how does this make people feel about it? Ahh well that changes it again.

Ok pause, lets compare the original design to where we are now.
