UI DESIGN

WEBSITE CONCEPT (Desktop)

The focus of this product is providing a service via website (Desktop Version). The user will be allowed to come online and have access to relevant resources on Pilates to help drive their fitness goals. The potential users would range from beginner to advance. Through my person research and reading, develop of UI design normally adopt the view of mobile first. However, I wanted to break this down into two separate projects, and to reflect individually on my learning experience.

VISUAL LANGUAGE

The visual language for the theme of this website, will build on the consistency and cohesion with Pilatesâ€™s theme. To communicate the services on the website, I will need to identify common links in Pilates, relevant to the font, space, shape, movement that could be highlighted and bring a sense of relevance and familiarity to the user. This would aim to engage with the user and follow the step outline on the website. To understand the visual language, I need to carry out a design inception worksheet.

DESIGN INCEPTION

Using the worksheet, I was able to refer to the user personas to build out association made with the service provided. Pilates is a muscles and strength-based exercise, so much of the association is with movement, space, and shape as this is reflected on the types of exercise done through Pilates. The second association was colour, much of Pilates is grounded by soft colours and reflective of lightness and peace - good colours associated with this service are pastel colours. This information brainstorms helps centred what the user expectation would be, this brings a sense of familiarity/interest in the user.

MOODBOARD

Using the associate key words, I went on to google to gather resources that would visual the mood of the website. I also wanted to validate whether the associations were true to the key message of what services would be provided. The mood board below, demonstrate the validity of the key words associated during the inception design worksheet.

STYLE TILE

Taking the mood board and now developing into a more detail version of what the website could look like. I have taken the shapes and colour, convert this into components to visual the arrangements of how the website would look and convey the message of Pilates, its purpose etc. I have focused on images and using shapes/curves to reflect the different exercise/fluidity, pastel colours/ opaqueness to demonstrate relaxation and peacefulness. This is always good to do, because it quick and easy to play around see what works.

STYLE GUIDE

The combination of the mood board and style tile has given clear direction of what type of messages are being conveyed thought the website, and it true to what the user would like to see. I have collated the information broken down into four distinct categories which will be later used to help build out the HF wireframe. Those categories are colour, typography, iconography, and UI components.

REFLECTION

Website UI design allows you think of space and how to use it. This can be great in one sense, because you fit various elements on the other hand, you must be careful not overwhelm the user with too much information or content. I guess with a mobile first approach, most the time you prioritise what elements are important and transfer that into a bigger screen and where necessary add in a few more elements. This was a good project, because it allowed me to see this sort of issues that could arise. The site map was particularly useful as to how I prioritized what was on the website, something I could use when working of client project. The other aspect that I found quite interest and unique to this project the reduce use of icons. I did go and research into other service website, and this was apparent â€“ again another insight I would not have thought about. If there was another element I would have added, it would have thought about user accessibility and how that would affect the current design I have created. It is something to return back and see what impact/different this would make to the user.
